Monica Shandal

PhD Candidate, Economics · Environmental & Development Economics

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of California, Santa Cruz.

My research lies at the intersection of environmental and development economics. I study the transboundary impacts of international waste trade, with a particular focus on how imported plastic waste affects local pollution exposure, health, and economic outcomes in Southeast Asia.

My work combines quasi-experimental methods with high-resolution spatial data, administrative records, and remote sensing to inform environmental policy and global trade regulation.

Plastic Waste, Trade Shocks, and Downwind Health

Job Market Paper

Exploits China’s 2018 National Sword policy as a quasi-experiment to estimate how rerouted plastic waste affects downwind PM2.5 and neonatal outcomes in Indonesia.

  • • Design: Event study, TWFE, geospatial exposure
  • • Data: Customs flows, Global Plastic Watch, PM2.5 rasters
